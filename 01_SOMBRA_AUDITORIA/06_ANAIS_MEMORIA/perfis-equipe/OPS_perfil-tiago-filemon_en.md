## 1. Professional Journey

Architect and urbanist specialized in vernacular bioconstruction and low-carbon construction techniques, Filemon Tiago built his trajectory through conviction rather than the market. He works exclusively with sustainable projects — prioritizing raw earth, bamboo, and local materials — and has a signature of execution in vulnerability contexts: urban occupations, riverine communities, stilt areas, settlements.

He masterfully masters adobe, wattle and daub, rammed earth, bamboo-a-pique, natural plaster, Peruvian quinta, and BTC. He has carried out projects in Amapá, Goiás, and the Federal District, always with a community mutirão (collective effort) methodology and technology transfer. He has an active network of technical partners in Goiânia (Prof. Rogério/UFG) and regional bamboo experts. Author of technical articles in partnership and a graduation thesis (TCC) featuring a project for community architecture for early childhood.

### Specific Contribution to the Platform

Responsible for the execution of the itinerate workshops of the **Flying Dome (Domo Voador)**, for the training of the first local multipliers of Takwara technology in Amazonian communities, and for the coordination of the Phase 2 construction-schools (canteiros-escola). He is the interface between the technical system developed in the laboratory and the hands that build in the territory. His experience with communities in vulnerable situations — where receptivity cannot be assumed, it must be earned — is an irreplaceable competence for the territorial implementation phase.

### Career Leap in the Platform

Filemon worked 90% with BTC due to market necessity, with the declared desire for it to be the reverse. The Platform offers exactly the reverse: the **Vegetable PU + bamboo residue + post-consumer PET brick** — stronger than concrete, without cement, without mortar — as the main product of an industrial system that he will not have to fund or justify alone. For the first time, the architect who chose communities when he could have chosen otherwise will have a dignified salary, a team, inputs, and scale for the work he always wanted to do.
