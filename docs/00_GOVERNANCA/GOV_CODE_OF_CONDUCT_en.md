## Our Commitment

The Regenerative Amazon Platform is an open science, social technology, and community bioeconomy project. Our community of collaborators — researchers, master builders, community leaders, managers, and technicians — shares the commitment to maintaining a **free, respectful, and productive** collaboration environment, regardless of origin, gender, ethnicity, technical experience, or institutional affiliation.

---

## Our Values

- 🎋 **Technological Sovereignty:** the knowledge generated here belongs to the communities that apply it.
- 🤝 **Fair Credit:** every contribution is recorded, cited, and recognized — no one disappears in the collective process.
- 🌍 **Open Science:** everything we produce is published under a CC BY 4.0 license — to be replicated, adapted, and improved.
- 👩 **Active Equity:** we prioritize the leadership of women, indigenous peoples, and historically excluded communities.
- 🔬 **Rigor with Humility:** we know that popular knowledge and technical-scientific knowledge complement each other.

---

## What We Expect from Each Collaborator

✅ Correctly cite sources and original authors in every contribution.

✅ Clearly identify what is your personal contribution and what is derived from other works.

✅ Open an Issue before proposing major structural changes — the community decides collectively.

✅ Communicate delays and unavailabilities — open collaboration depends on transparency.

✅ Treat all contributors with respect, regardless of technical level or institutional affiliation.

---

## What We Do Not Tolerate

❌ Appropriating others' contributions without attribution of credit.

❌ Submitting project documents to calls for proposals or publications without informing and crediting the other authors.

❌ Using the repository for purposes that conflict with the Platform's socio-environmental principles.

❌ Any form of discrimination or harassment.

## Contributions and Authorship

Every contribution accepted via Pull Request generates permanent credit in the repository history and, when relevant, in the `.zenodo.json` file for academic citation purposes.

The Platform adopts the **CC BY 4.0** model: you can use, adapt, and redistribute any content from this repository as long as you cite the source and the authors.
