## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Modern civil construction demands resilient, low-carbon alternatives to replace steel and concrete. Bamboo emerges as an extremely high-performance structural material, yet its Achilles' heel has historically been its **connections**.

As highlighted by the systematic review [(Adier et al., 2023)](https://doi.org/10.3390/buildings13102449), one of the greatest gaps in the structural use of bamboo lies in the need to explore **sustainable filling materials** that serve as alternatives to anchoring with concrete and mortar. The use of traditional cementitious materials, besides having a high environmental impact, compromises the natural ductility of the bamboo, creating rigid points that lead to cracking under thermal or mechanical stress.

The **Takwara Construction System** solves this gap by replacing constrictive rigid elements with a cohesive arrangement focused on the so-called "vegetal weld", combining treated bamboo with **Vegetal Polyurethane (PU)** to ensure flexible and durable structural fixation.

## 2. Mechanical Performance and Resistance

The reliability of the connections and the viability of the sandwich panels are directly supported by the remarkable physical-mechanical properties of bamboo, frequently compared to or exceeding conventional materials:

*   **Tensile Strength:** The longitudinal fibers, densely packed in the bamboo culm, confer formidable tensile strength. According to the systematic review [(Adier et al., 2023)](https://doi.org/10.3390/buildings13102449), structural species like the Asian giant *Phyllostachys edulis* (Moso) show tensile support between 147 and 423 MPa, while *Guadua angustifolia* (predominant in the Americas) averages 226 MPa, surpassing the performance of most hardwoods.
*   **Compressive Strength:** Still according to [(Adier et al., 2023)](https://doi.org/10.3390/buildings13102449), testing (following premises of international standards like ISO 22157 and NBR 16828) indicates compressive strength up to 220 MPa for *Guadua*. These figures are substantially higher than conventional fast-growing reforestation species. This exceptional balance under compressive load is a direct reflection of its unique lignocellulosic microstructure, whose graded fiber distribution optimizes the absorption of mechanical impacts superior to commercial wood [(Qiu et al., 2020)](#).
*   **Density and Lightness:** The density of *Guadua* (ranging from 500 to 1100 kg/m³) provides one of the best strength-to-weight ratios in civil engineering, a central aspect for prefabricated use (such as the Lego model of HIS panels).

Premature failure in traditional typologies rarely results from the breaking of the bamboo culm itself, but rather from shear induced in the nodes and rigid connections — which is why the development of the union with Vegetal PU is mandatory for structural safety. By eliminating these conventional bottlenecks, the system responds to the application challenges pointed out in recent decades by researchers, who attest to the full viability, strength, and advantageous ecological and economic relationship of bamboo as a substitute for steel and traditional masonry in civil and residential construction [(Paiva & Tannuri, 2004)](#); [(Gonçalves et al., 2009)](#); [(Silva et al., 2010)](#).

## 3. "Vegetal Welding" and PU Technology

The essence of the structural union in the Takwara System does not rely on literal welding by melting metals, but on the exceptional mechanical and chemical adhesion of Vegetal PU to the bamboo fiber. The system employs two complementary formulations:

*   **Expansive Vegetal PU (such as MAMONEX® RD70):** Injected into the internodes and empty spaces of the connections. It expands, filling organic grooves and interlocking structural cables or dowels in a solidary manner, but without the friable rigidity of concrete.
*   **Waterproofing Vegetal PU (such as UG 132-A):** Applied externally to cover, seal, and protect joints and exposed surfaces, conferring extremely high resistance to moisture, weathering, and biodeterioration.

### 3.1 The Interdependence of Thermal Treatment (Thermal Rectification)

For the absolute success of the Vegetal PU anchoring, the bamboo requires physical preparation: it must be permanently dry, and the joining interfaces must be clean or sanded, ensuring full chemical affinity and penetration of the polymer into the fibers.

In this sense, the construction technology inherently depends on the preliminary process of **thermal rectification** at the biorefinery. In this ecological thermal treatment, the bamboo is exposed to saturated high-temperature steam (often enriched with pyroligneous acid). This heating not only accelerates the drying of the culm — enabling long-term storage and predictability of the production line — but also physically alters the cell wall, drastically reducing hemicellulose and starch levels. The result is the documented gain of maximum dimensional stability and biological invulnerability [(Chung & Wang, 2017)](#); [(Wang et al., 2020)](#). Only after this stage is the bamboo strictly ready to receive the vegetal PU weld.

### 3.2 Biological Shielding with Pyroligneous Acid

Integrated with the thermal process, the use of **pyroligneous acid** (the liquid product resulting from the condensation of organic pyrolysis smoke) elevates the bamboo's protection to a state of prolonged immunity. Rich in phenolic compounds and organic acids, saturated steam or a bath with bamboo vinegar acts as a highly efficient, natural preservative, fungicide, bactericide, and insecticide.

Studies on the performance of this byproduct confirm that immersing bamboo splints in such acidic solutions guarantees real protection against biodecomposition and the fatal attack of the bamboo borer (*Dinoderus minutus*), efficiently replacing the strong toxic and carbon footprint left by conventional chemical salts and baths on the market [(Silva, 2011)](#). The combination of structural thermal alignment with the saturation of the pyroligneous seal provides the piece with full primary safety so that the definitive enclosure can take place with the castor oil film in HIS beams.

## 4. Connections for Large-Scale Structures (Beams, Trusses, and Rafters)

For sheds, bridges, large-span roofs (like trusses and rafters), or simply supported beams, the perpendicular or parallel jointing of bamboo poles requires extra attention to shear and friction.

**The Problem with Rigid Metals:** The direct insertion of heavy through-bolts, thick threaded rods, and other rigid metallic elements creates constant, concentrated mechanical friction. This invariably leads to **longitudinal cracks in the bamboo**, drastically reducing the structure's load-bearing capacity.

**The Takwara Solution:**
To circumvent the risk of cracking, the system eliminates rigid metal pins in favor of a fibrous and flexible integration:

1.  **Splint Bundles:** Instead of piercing steel through the bamboo, bundles of bamboo splints (longitudinal strips highly resistant to traction) are tightly bound, wrapping around and embracing the unified culms.
2.  **Locking Wedges (Tabiques):** Small transverse battens ("tabiques") made from dense bamboo itself are inserted into staggered millings.
3.  **Expansive PU Matrix:** The entire internode subjected to the stress node is filled with expansive PU. The PU seals the bundle of splints, the tied cable, and the wedges, forming a complete, solidary, non-friable composite "node".

## 5. Connections for Curved and Semi-flexible Structures (Geodesic Domes)

In the context of geodesic domes, where dozens of culms converge dynamically forming complex nodes, Takwara Technology utilizes an **internally pre-tensioned steel cable system with thimbles**.

Instead of the abrasion from a rigid metal cap, a thick, flexible steel cable (1m long) internally traverses the ends through four angled holes. Upon exiting the pole through a finishing washer ("plug"), the cable forms a loop with a terminal (thimble). This flexibility allows it to perfectly adapt to frequencies v2, v3, v4, etc.

*   **Final Fixation:** Once the triangles are assembled, high-density Vegetal PU is injected throughout the entire end of the pole, engulfing the internally hidden cable and internal clamp, permanently sealing the mechanism ("vegetal weld"). Externally, the metallic face of the washer is coated with a lamination of PU and sisal fiber.

## 6. HIS Sandwich Panels and Biocomposites (The "Lego" Project)

The vision of bioconstruction culminates in enclosing the structure and providing building infrastructure through biocomposites — a direct application combining bamboo, waste, and Vegetal PU.

This focus is the heart of the project for **Social Interest Housing (HIS)**.

### 6.1 Sandwich Panel System
Unlike structure made of poles, the walls and claddings consist of **Intelligent Sandwich Panels**.
*   **Components:** Using a custom lathe, dry bamboo (pre-treated with pyroligneous steam) has its inner nodes removed and is sliced into laths of uniform width. Multiple laths are aligned, covered with successive layers of Waterproofing PU and a reinforcing mesh (fabric or fiber), forming the "skins" or faces of the panel.
*   **The Solidary Core:** Two faces of these sheets are paired around the structure or predefined molds. The internal core space is then structurally filled by the **Expansive Vegetal PU**, solidifying both faces in the same resilient manner.

### 6.2 "Lego" Modularity and Elimination of Conventional Mortar
The panels and blocks operate in a strictly scalable format, custom-manufactured as interlocking pieces (the "Lego" model):
*   **Modular Dimensions:** Designed in sizes up to **3x3 meters**, they are easy to lift, transport to the site, and attach to the primary infrastructure.
*   **Prefabricated Openings:** The main innovation of the Intelligent Panel is that it leaves the production line already with pre-molded and machined matrices: **sized openings to accommodate doors and windows**.
*   **Embedded Ducts:** Prior to injecting the expansive core PU, all electrical conduit, plumbing, and data tubing are internally secured to the organic faces. When the PU inflates and cures, the ducts are permanently enveloped and hermetically locked in place, eliminating any need to "break walls" on site.
*   **Interlocking Bricks and Blocks:** For smaller enclosures, the technology expands into biocomposite bricks (agglomerating waste and PU). The main proposal of the system is to **eliminate the traditional 3x1 laying mortar (3 parts sand to 1 part cement)**, using cords of the Vegetal PU itself to glue and lock the structural blocks cleanly, swiftly, and without water.

### 6.3 Cardboard and Sand Composite Roof Tiles
Beyond vertical enclosure, the system addresses sustainable roofing. **Composite tiles made from recycled cardboard pulp or repurposed cellulose** are employed. The cardboard is molded, saturated with Waterproofing Vegetal PU, and receives a sprinkling of **sand** before final curing. The sand provides superior mechanical resistance against hail and weathering (UV protection), drastically elevating the thermal and acoustic comfort of the building in areas of extreme sun exposure.

## 7. Final Considerations

The **Structural Connections and Bamboo Biocomposites System** redefines the contemporary use of natural-based forestry technology. From the delicate geometric interconnection of domes via embedded steel cables, to the pioneering replacement of concrete and heavy metals with bamboo tenons locked in mamonex polyurethane, Brazil inaugurates a new benchmark at the forefront of Architecture and Engineering. The Intelligent Sandwich Panels consolidate the necessary path: the rapid industrialization of organic matter to generate large-scale housing in a sustainable, swift manner without the waste of cementitious inputs.
