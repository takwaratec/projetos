### To Be or Not To Be

> Free adaptation of William Shakespeare's monologue — Hamlet, 1599

### To Be or Not to Be an Activist? That is the Question:

Whether 'tis nobler in our spirit to suffer the tricks with which enraged politics targets us, or to rise up against this sea of corruption and by opposing end them?

To vote... to like: no more. To say that with a vote we end the heartache and the thousand social struggles—heritage of Brazilians:

To vote to like... 'tis a consummation devoutly to be rejected with fervor. To like... perchance to share: Ay, there's the disaster:

For when wrapped in campaign discourse, at the Nation's command, every excrescence uttered and decreed makes us applaud:

There's the stupidity that makes calamity of so long life to our misfortunes.

Who would bear the inapetence of managers, the bargaining of positions, 38 ministries, all the benefits and perks, besides high salaries, privileged forum, more than a hundred amendments to the constitution, official insolence, which differentiates them from all before the Law. Who would bear it, when he might his most perfect administration make with Artificial Intelligence?

Who would fardels bear, to grunt and sweat under a weary life, if the principle of Ethics,—undiscovered country of these heralds, whose spelling was erased from the regulations—did not place us in a position of justice and equality?

Omission thus makes cowards of us all, and through the misuse of technology and knowledge such abuses are allowed, with the cynical and sick tone of blind militancy; and since such aberrations bind us, High-influence Parties without scruples negotiate amendments and commissions, defining policies increasingly distant from true democracy.
