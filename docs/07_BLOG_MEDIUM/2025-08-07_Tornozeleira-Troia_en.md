### A critical article on the Brazilian crossroads and the future of humanity.

**Brasília, July 18, 2025** — While Brazil was distracted by the farcical spectacle of the "Trojan Ankle Monitor," a judicial drama with a reality show script, an older and much darker script was being fulfilled. It was not written in Brasília offices or newsrooms, but decades earlier, in the pages of philosophers like [Slavoj Žižek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavoj_%C5%BDi%C5%BEek). What we are living through is not news; it is a rerun. And the programming that follows, as economists like [Richard Wolff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_D._Wolff) and [Yanis Varoufakis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yanis_Varoufakis) warn, is the transition to a future that resembles a feudal past, now digitized.

The scene is almost too perfect in its irony: in the dead of night, hours before the exact moment when the nation would drown in the enjoyment of televised political revenge, the National Congress, acting as the council of elders of a decaying empire, advanced with [PL 2159/21](https://www.camara.leg.br/noticias/1181164-camara-aprova-projeto-que-altera-regras-de-licenciamento-ambiental/). The amnesty for deforesters and the flexibilization of environmental licensing were not an accident on the path. They were the logical climax of a play whose plot Žižek had already dissected in works like [In Defense of Lost Causes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In_Defense_of_Lost_Causes) (2015): contemporary ideology does not work by deceiving us with promises, but by distracting us with "jouissance" (enjoyment) so that we do not see the catastrophe we ourselves engender.

#### Enjoyment as a Smokescreen: A Žižekian Script

Žižek's philosophy offers us the key to deciphering this play. Toxic polarization, symbolized by the electronic ankle monitor, is not a sign of system dysfunction; it is its **ideal mode of operation**. It generates a constant flow of enjoyment (jouissance) that paralyzes critical thinking. On one side, the sadistic enjoyment of seeing the old power humiliated. On the other, the masochistic enjoyment of identifying with the persecuted martyr.

This "passion for the Real," as Žižek would say, quoting [Alain Badiou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alain_Badiou), is a passion for a false Real, a simulacrum that prevents us from seeing the traumatic Real of our condition. The ankle monitor is the Trojan Horse: a flashy gift that, once accepted in our media living room, releases the soldiers who will plunder our commons—our forests, our water, our future.

**Welcome to Technofeudalism, Brazil: The Double Vassalage**

This dynamic of distraction and plunder is not an exclusively Brazilian phenomenon. It is the modus operandi of the new global order that Yanis Varoufakis baptized as **Technofeudalism.** As detailed in the report [Technofeudalism in Brazil](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/O%20Tecnofeudalismo%20no%20Brasil/), the thesis is brutally simple: capitalism, as we knew it, is dead. Big Techs no longer compete in a market; they are the market. They are digital fiefdoms where we, the "cloud serfs," work for free generating data, while traditional companies become "vassal capitalists," paying tributes to access their own clients.

In this scenario, Brazil finds itself in the worst possible position: that of **"double vassalage."** Historically a vassal of the North American ecosystem, it now finds itself strategically penetrated by Chinese technological power. We are an extraction territory for two competing feudal lords, a condition that drains our resources and undermines our sovereignty.

**The Symptom of [Bamboo](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/A2.%20%20O%20Bambu/a2.1%20Vis%C3%A3o%20Geral/): The Screaming Omission**

And here is where the performance of the Ministry of the Environment (MMA) becomes central. The [COP30 Dossier](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/A6.%20COP30/a5.0%20Dossi%C3%AA%20COP30/) report denounces an omission so blatant that it can only be intentional. Brazil holds the [largest native bamboo forest](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/A2.%20%20O%20Bambu/a1.0%20O%20Papel%20do%20Bambu%20nas%20Queimadas/) in the world, a plant that is a true bioeconomy platform, capable of driving everything from soil [**phytoremediation**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/A2.%20%20O%20Bambu/a2.6%20Fitorremedia%C3%A7%C3%A3o/) to food security and sustainable [**civil construction**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/A2.%20%20O%20Bambu/a2.2%20Caracteriza%C3%A7%C3%A3o%20F%C3%ADsica/).

The technology, as demonstrated by the [**Bio-Green and Yellow Revolution**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/ha-solucao/) project, already exists. By combining castor oil polyurethane—whose Brazilian pioneering is detailed in [**The Mine Map**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/Arando-Rolls-Royce/)—with bamboo and waste, Brazil could lead a revolution in sustainable architecture, as proposed by architects Sérgio Prado and Márcia Macul in [**The Vanguard of Circular Architecture**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/arquitetura-resistencia/). This platform could resolve the [**housing deficit**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/A6.%20COP30/a5.2%20Deficit%20Habitacional/) and overcome the colonial logic of concrete, criticized in [**The Concrete Longhouse (Maloca)**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/maloca-concreto/) and [**ATHIS: Colonialism and Resistance**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/athis-concreto-bambu/).

Why, then, does the MMA, in its grandiloquent plans for the bioeconomy, ignore bamboo, even when there is a National Policy (Law No. 12,484/2011) languishing for lack of regulation? The answer, through Žižek's lens, is that bamboo is the **symptom**: the repressed truth of a system that prefers technological dependency and the profit of established lobbies over national sovereignty and community autonomy. The omission is not inertia; it is a political act.

#### A Call to Insurrection: Beyond Vassalage

Žižek's "impossible solution" is not a reform, but a refoundation. It's not going to Mars, but confronting the disaster here on Earth, guided by the [**Climate Ultimatum**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/Ultimato-Clim%C3%A1tico/) that science imposes on us.

**This article is a public appeal. An appeal for Brazilian civil society to awaken from the trance of the spectacle. The true threat to the nation is not on the ankles of politicians, but in the laws that are passed while we look away.**

It is time to direct our indignation to the competent channels, transforming passive enjoyment into a political act:

* **To the Civil House of the Presidency of the Republic:** We demand a full presidential veto of [**PL 2159/21**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/o-pl-da-devastacao/).
* **To the Ministry of Justice and Public Safety:** We demand an investigation into the predatory lobby that transforms the [**public interest into a commodity**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/A6.%20COP30/a5.0%20Dossi%C3%AA%20COP30/).
* **To the National Congress, via the Senate e-Cidadania portal and Chamber of Deputies channels:** We demand the [**immediate regulation of Law No. 12,484/2011**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/), the National Bamboo Policy, and an honest debate about a development model that includes endogenous solutions, such as those proposed in the report on [**Agrarian Reform and National Technological Sovereignty**](https://resck.github.io/Takwara-Tech/reforma-agraria/).

Brazil can continue as a vassal in the cynical script of technofeudalism, entertained by its own dramas while its future is plundered. Or it can perform the impossible act: tear up the script, confront its symptoms, and start building a Real sovereignty. The choice, as always, is ours. But time is running out.
