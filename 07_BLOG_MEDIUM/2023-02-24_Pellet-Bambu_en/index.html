    Biomass Converted into Carbon Neutral Energy

    Originally published on Medium on 2023-02-24. Integrated into the structured technical archive (Advocacy 5.1).

    Biomass: The Renewable Frontier of Energy, Food, and Sustainable Innovation

    Biomass, defined as all renewable organic matter resources used for food production, thermoelectric energy, or fuels, comes from diverse sources. This includes animal waste, forest, agricultural, industrial, and urban residues. Among the ways to use biomass, briquettes and pellets emerge as solutions with extremely high energy potential, promoting not only comfort and prosperity but also the regeneration of wealth.

    In China, innovation led to the development of "Bamllets," briquettes and pellets manufactured from bamboo, while globally they are known as Wood Pellets or simply Briquettes. These are types of machined charcoal, produced from the compression of organic matter, usable both for energy generation and animal feed.

    Differences and Production Processes

    Briquettes can range from 6 cm to 20 cm in diameter and up to 40 cm in length, while pellets, or pellets, do not exceed 3 x 4 cm, with minimum dimensions of 2 mm x 2 mm. The production of these biofuels involves densification and compression under high pressures and temperatures, resulting in a product with low moisture, high density, and calorific value, in addition to slow burning and low emissions.

    History and Evolution

    The history of briquettes dates back to 1897, with the first registered patent and the first factories emerging in the US after the First World War. Interestingly, in 1920, Henry Ford created the first vegetable fiber briquette, the "Ford Charcoal," using wood scraps from his factory, a precursor to today's sustainable fuels. Pellets, in turn, emerged in the 1970s in Sweden, representing an evolution of briquettes in terms of handling, storage, and automation.

    Activated Carbon: Expanding the Use of Biomass

    Activated carbon, derived from the carbonization and activation of biomass such as bamboo, has extensive applications in water and air purification, waste treatment, and other industrial applications. This product takes advantage of bamboo's rapid growth rate and regeneration capacity, contributing to sustainable waste management and treatment solutions.

    The Role of Bamboo in Biomass Production

    Bamboo stands out for its unique characteristics, such as uniformity, strength, density, and high calorific value, making it a promising material for the production of ENplus® class B pellets. The exploitation of bamboo in Brazil, especially in the cellulose sector, reveals the untapped potential of this biomass.

    Future Perspectives

    The use of biomass, through the production of briquettes, pellets, and activated carbon, offers a unique opportunity to promote sustainability and innovation. Brazil, with its rich biodiversity and vast forest cover, is positioned to lead the production of solid biofuels and activated carbon, contributing to sustainable economic development and the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions. Awareness and the adoption of incentive public policies are essential to unlock biomass's potential as an innovative and sustainable solution that meets contemporary energy and environmental demands.

    Investment Costs

    The implementation cost of a wood or bamboo pellet industry can vary significantly depending on the scale and technology adopted. An estimated initial investment is around R$ 2.5 million. This value can cover everything from land acquisition and facility construction to the purchase of specific equipment for pellet or briquette production. It is important to note that the return on this investment can be quite fast, depending on the production volume and market strategies adopted.

    Financing Channels

    There are several financing options available in Brazil for biomass and renewable energy projects, including:

    • National Bank for Economic and Social Development (BNDES): Offers financing lines for renewable energy projects, including biomass, with favorable payment and interest conditions.
    • Agency for Studies and Projects (FINEP): Supports technological innovation projects in renewable energy through financing and grants.
    • Commercial Banks and Credit Cooperatives: Some banks have special credit lines for renewable energy and can offer attractive conditions for projects in this area.
    • Climate Fund: Managed by the Ministry of the Environment, offers resources for projects that contribute to the mitigation of climate change, including the use of biomass.

    Industry Associations

    • Brazilian Clean Energy Association (ABEeólica): Although focused on wind energy, it also represents renewable energy interests in general.
    • Brazilian Association of Biomass and Renewable Energy Industries (ABIB): Represents companies and promotes the development of the biomass sector in Brazil.
    • Brazilian Pellet Association (ABPEL): Specifically focused on the pellet sector, works to promote the use of biomass as an energy source and develop the national and international market.

    Industry Companies in Brazil

    • Eucatex: One of the leaders in the biomass sector, using eucalyptus waste to produce energy.
    • GranBio: Innovative in the bioenergy sector, develops technologies for the production of advanced biofuels.
    • Suzano: In addition to being one of the largest paper and pulp producers, invests in biomass projects for energy.
    • Bamboo Energy: Company specializing in the production of renewable energy from bamboo, standing out for its innovation and sustainability.

    Conclusion

    The biomass and renewable energy sector in Brazil presents a promising scenario, with several investment and financing opportunities. The support of associations and the presence of innovative companies in the market contribute to the sustainable development and diversification of the country's energy matrix. It is essential for those interested in investing in this sector to perform a detailed analysis of financing options and partnerships, as well as stay updated on market trends and regulations.
