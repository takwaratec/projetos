**A Milestone for Valuation and Sustainable Growth in Brazil**

### Bamboo Parliamentary Front:

#### A Milestone for Valuation and Sustainable Growth in Brazil

> Despite its historical presence and recognized versatility, bamboo has walked at a slow pace in the Brazilian economic and political scenario. However, the recent awakening of the National Congress to the potential of this renewable resource is a breath of fresh air for entrepreneurs in the sector. The formation of the Parliamentary Front for Bamboo and Natural Fibers signals a promising movement towards the constitution of a serious incentive policy for bamboo in Brazil.

![](https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/1*YnoTUsON4FpRzJaIwhZ88w.jpeg)

Bamboo, a fast-growing plant with remarkable properties, has been underutilized in Brazil despite its historical presence and economic potential. However, the recent mobilization of the National Congress, with the formation of the Mixed Parliamentary Front for Bamboo and Natural Fibers, represents a significant step towards the valuation and sustainable growth of the sector. This initiative promises to be a game-changer for entrepreneurs and researchers who see in bamboo an opportunity for economic development aligned with environmental sustainability.

The launching ceremony of the Mixed Parliamentary Front for Bamboo and Natural Fibers took place on August 30, 2023, bringing together various key actors, including government representatives, experts, and entities in the sector. It is composed of parliamentarians from the Chamber of Deputies and the Federal Senate, and its specific purposes include discussing public policies, integrating with government actions and civil entities, stimulating and disseminating academic and scientific research on bamboo, and promoting its use in strategies to combat climate degradation.

#### The Underutilization of the National Bamboo Policy: A Critical Analysis of Party and Government Composition in the Parliamentary Front

In 2011, Law No. 12,484 was sanctioned, which instituted the National Policy to Encourage Sustainable Management and Cultivation of Bamboo (PNMCB). The law promised to be a milestone for sustainable development and social inclusion, given bamboo's versatility and economic potential. However, more than a decade later, the effective implementation of this policy remains largely on paper, and the composition of the Bamboo and Natural Fibers Parliamentary Front reveals a worrying absence of members from the government base, which may be contributing to the sector's stagnation.

**Absence of the Government Base:** An analysis of the party composition of the Bamboo Parliamentary Front demonstrates a predominance of the Liberal Party (PL), with approximately 51.9% of the members. While party plurality is healthy in a democracy, the significant absence of members from the government base in the Bamboo Parliamentary Front is an indication that the mission to implement the PNMCB is not receiving the necessary attention from key political actors with decision-making power.

**The Importance of Government Participation:** Leveraging the bamboo sector in Brazil intrinsically depends on the active participation of the Ministries of Education, Environment, Science and Technology, Labor, and Social Development. These government bodies hold the keys to unlocking bamboo's potential on several fronts: research and development, environmental conservation, job creation, and social inclusion. The lack of activism and coordinated action between these ministries and the Bamboo Parliamentary Front results in a lost opportunity for the country.

**Allocation of Resources and Social Development:** The already scarce resources destined for the implementation of the PNMCB are often diverted to areas that, although important, have a limited impact on the emerging social development that bamboo could provide. Bamboo has the potential to be a vector for economic transformation, especially for rural and marginalized populations, but without the government's support and strategic vision, this potential remains underutilized.

**Current Political Scenario:** The Mixed Parliamentary Front for Bamboo and Natural Fibers has the support of several parties, reflecting a multi-party interest in sustainable development and the valuation of bamboo in Brazil.

**PL** (Liberal Party), with an expressive representation of 51%, has an extensive list of parliamentarians engaged in the cause of bamboo and natural fibers. Among them are Giovani Cherini, Vicentinho Júnior, Vinicius Carvalho, Vitor Lippi, Wilson Santiago, among others, who demonstrate the party's strong commitment to sustainable development and the valuation of bamboo in Brazil.

**REPUBLICANOS**, with 8.5% representation, includes parliamentarians such as Silvia Cristina, Silvia Waiãpi, and Sostenes Cavalcante, showing the party's engagement with green agendas.

**MDB** (Brazilian Democratic Movement) presents 7.5% participation, with members like Baleia Rossi and Celso Maldaner, who bring their experiences and visions on bamboo's potential to the table.

**PSDB** (Party of Brazilian Social Democracy), with 5.5%, includes names like Rodrigo de Castro and Samuel Moreira, reflecting the party's interest in promoting a greener and more innovative economy.

**DEM** (Democrats), with 4.5%, has representatives like Elmar Nascimento and Paulo Azi, who contribute to the diversity of ideas and actions within the Parliamentary Front.

**PP** (Progressives), with 4%, has the participation of parliamentarians like Arthur Lira and Ricardo Barros, both with a history of support for initiatives aiming at economic development allied to sustainability.

**PDT** (Democratic Labor Party), with 3.5%, brings names like Tábata Amaral and Subtenente Gonzaga, who have stood out for their progressive stances regarding the environment.

**PT** (Workers' Party), with 3%, has parliamentarians such as José Guimarães and Patrus Ananias, who have historically aligned with environmental and social causes.

**PSB** (Brazilian Socialist Party), with 3%, has representatives like Júlio Delgado and Tadeu Alencar, who reinforce the party's commitment to sustainability.

**PSL** (Social Liberal Party), with 3%, includes names like Delegado Waldir and Julian Lemos, who also join the collective effort for the valuation of bamboo.

**NOVO**, with 2%, brings parliamentarians like Marcel van Hattem and Paulo Ganime, who are known for their liberal stances and support for innovative solutions for sustainable development.

**PSD** (Social Democratic Party), with 2%, has the participation of Diego Andrade and Sidney Leite, who add to the dialogue on the sustainable use of natural resources.

**PODE** (Podemos), with 2%, has parliamentarians like Renata Abreu and José Nelto, who have shown openness to discussing and supporting initiatives that promote the use of bamboo.

**PSOL** (Socialism and Liberty Party), with 1%, has the presence of Sâmia Bomfim and Glauber Braga, both with a history of environmental defense and sustainable practices.

**PROS** (Republican Party of Social Order), with 1%, includes names like Capitão Wagner and Vaidon Oliveira, who bring to the Parliamentary Front a vision focused on social and environmental progress.

**PSC** (Social Christian Party), with 1%, has representatives like Otoni de Paula and André Ferreira, who join the effort to boost the bamboo sector in the country.

And finally, **PCdoB** (Communist Party of Brazil), with 1%, has parliamentarians like Orlando Silva and Daniel Almeida, who defend the development of a fairer and more sustainable economy.

**Conclusion:** The Bamboo and Natural Fibers Parliamentary Front needs an injection of party diversity and a stronger presence from the government base, notably from parties traditionally involved with social and environmental causes, so that the PNMCB leaves the paper and becomes a transformative reality. It is imperative that relevant ministries actively engage in the mission to promote bamboo as a strategic crop for sustainable development and social inclusion in Brazil. Only with a collaborative and inter-ministerial approach can we expect to see the bamboo sector flourish and fulfill the promises of its founding legislation.
